PLIC: A Parallel Lightweight Image Compression Method for High-Bandwidth Low-Latency Remote Desktop
Zhankun Zhang, Jinyu Wang, Weiguo Wu, Zhike Li, Chi Zhang, Chunmiao Ma, and Yang Guo
In Proc. of IEEE DCC, 2024
Acceptance Rate: 15.1% (15 out of 98)

HTML PDF

In high bandwidth scenarios, this research addresses the problem of presenting desktop images, such as real-time and clarity, for remote desktop systems. Remote desktop tools typically introduce image compression to manage bandwidth. Because of the excellent performance in eliminating redundancy of images, JPEG becomes a popular choice in remote desktop systems. While it creates artifacts and more computation as compression increases. Recently, high bandwidth environments have been gaining popularity, such as FTTR and FTTH, making the lightweight image compression possible. This research introduces a parallel lightweight image compression method. By reconstructing the coding process of JPEG, parallel techniques are utilized to accelerate the coding process to reduce the single-frame computational latency and improve the image quality. The method outperforms conventional JPEG and the latest lightweight compression JPEG XS (ISO/IEC 21122) standard in terms of single-frame latency, and it is visually lossless.
